About the Process

Motivation

Before you throw all your clothes into the washing machine, do you sort your clothes “whites with whites, darks with darks” to prevent staining? But, what happens to that residue in the water as it leaves the washing machine?

The overuse of synthetic dyes in the fast fashion industry has a huge toll on our environment. It is estimated that about 200 000 tons of synthetic dyes are released into the waterway every year. Because of their stubborn, resistant properties to high temperatures, chemicals, and detergents, it is quite difficult to treat dyes properly. Eventually it is released into natural waterways, contributing to water pollution, soil degradation, and disruption to aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Dyeing

Below is the methodology we created with through experimentation:

1.) Hydrate fabric in water, so fibres relax and take in the dyestuff
2.) Wash food scraps to remove residue/dirt/flesh
3.) Bring water to a boil
4.) Place rinsed food scraps in pot, lower heat to let simmer for 1 hour or until desired colour is achieved.
5.) Filter out food scraps by pouring liquid through a sieve into a bowl. Let cool to 30-40 degrees Celsius (if you don’t have a thermometer, you can touch the bowl and it should be warm.)
6.) Submerge hydrated fabric until desired colour is achieved (1 hour to overnight: note that drying and washing lightens colour)
7.) Rinse with cold water, hang dry. Hand wash gently and leave out of sunlight.
*** Discard of food scraps in the compost, and you can pour out the dye in the sink. ***Note that a mordant/modifier could be applied before, during, or after dyeing.


***Colours may differ depending on factors such as temperature and freshness of ingredients and scraps
